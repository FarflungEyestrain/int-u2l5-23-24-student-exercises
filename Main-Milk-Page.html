What is Milk?

Milk, a quintessential emulsion of nutritional bounty, stands as a paradigmatic fluid of sustenance, rich in a myriad of bioactive compounds. This opalescent liquid, secreted by the mammary glands of mammals, boasts a complex composition encompassing essential macronutrients—proteins, fats, and carbohydrates—alongside a plethora of micronutrients such as vitamins and minerals.

The protean nature of milk, manifesting in diverse forms from colostrum to matured dairy products, underscores its versatility and indispensability in human alimentation. Its inherent bioavailability and nutrient density render milk an unparalleled alimentary elixir, pivotal in the fortification of health and the sustenance of life.

3. Milk contains about 0.03 becquerels of radiation per liter.
